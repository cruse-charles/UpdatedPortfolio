Building A Real App #1

Follow along with my journey to build a real app

February 24, 2025

What Am I Building?

For the first time, I'm building a full-stack application that's not just a personal project, it's something I'm designing to use at work and (hopefully) share with the wider world of education consulting.

The Problem

At my job, education consultants spend hours organizing tasks, documents, and updates for each student. There's a lot of manual work — spreadsheets to track assignments, back-and-forth messages to confirm submission status, and custom reports written from scratch for progress check-ins. It's inefficient and repetitive. And it's exactly the kind of problem software is built to solve.

The MVP: Automating Task Organization

The first version of this app is focused entirely on one goal: making student task management easy. Here's what the MVP includes:

  1. Consultant View: Upload assignments or documents for students to complete.
  2. Student View: View tasks, upload submissions, and stay up-to-date.
  3. Automated Progress Tracking: Once a student submits their work, it's automatically flagged as completed. No more digging through email threads or checking off rows in spreadsheets.
  4. Clean UI: A central dashboard where consultants can see who's behind, who's on track, and what needs follow-up.
The goal is to eliminate redundant communication and reduce the administrative overhead. Everything will be tracked in one place, and everyone will stay in sync without constant reminders.

Building with Next.js and Firebase

To get this out the door quickly, I'm using:

  • Next.js for the frontend and server-side logic
  • Firebase for authentication, storage, and Firestore (real-time database)
Firebase is new for me, but it's fast to set up and handles a lot of boilerplate I'd otherwise need to build myself.

What Comes After MVP?

Once the task management system is stable, I'll be adding an analytics dashboard. This will automatically generate charts and data summaries that show how students are progressing. Right now, consultants at my job have to build reports manually — and it's time-consuming. With this feature, the app can generate visual reports for internal check-ins or meetings with parents.

Scraping University Data — Later

A longer-term goal is to implement university website scraping. The idea is that consultants can attach a school to a student's profile, and the app will fetch deadlines, essay prompts, and other relevant data automatically. This will be the most technically complex part of the project, especially since it may involve scraping dozens of different university sites. Because of that, I'm saving it for last.

But it's also the feature I see the most potential in for future growth. If I ever want to monetize this tool beyond my current job, building a database of clean, regularly updated college info might be the key.

What's Next

Right now, I'm laser-focused on getting the task management flow working end-to-end. That means:

  • Consultant uploads a task.
  • Student sees and completes it.
  • The app automatically updates their status.
  • Everyone can breathe a little easier.
This is the first time I'm building a real product to solve a real workflow problem, and I'm excited to ship fast, learn along the way, and hopefully make my coworkers' lives a little easier.
